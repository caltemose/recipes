Cauliflower and Cheddar Cheese Soup

Williams-Sonoma Vegetarian

Ingredients

  • 2 head(s) cauliflower small heads, 2-1/2 pounds total weight
  • 2 tablespoon(s) butter
  • 1 unit(s) yellow onion(s) chopped
  • 1 clove(s) garlic minced
  • 4 cup(s) vegetable stock
  • 1 teaspoon(s) dry mustard
  • 1 pinch nutmeg
  • 1.5 cup(s) milk
  • 2 cup(s) cheddar sharp, white, grated
  • 2 tablespoon(s) green onion sliced

Directions

  1. Trim the cauliflower. Cut one of the heads into 1/2-inch florets. Fill a saucepan three-fourths full with water, salt it lightly and bring to a boil over high heat. Add the florets, reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer until tender when pierced with a fork, 10-12 minutes. Drain well; set aside. Cut the remaining cauliflower head into 2-inch pieces; set aside.
  2. In a 4-quart soup pot over medium-low heat, melt the butter. Add the onion and sate, stirring, until soft, about 10 minutes. Add the garlic and sauce, stirring, for 2 minutes longer.
  3. Add the stock or water, reserved raw cauliflower pieces, dry mustard and nutmeg and bring to a boil over high heat. Reduce the heat to low and simmer, uncovered, until the cauliflower is soft, about 30 minutes. Remove from the heat and cool slightly.
  4. Working with 2 cups of soup at a time, place in a blender and blend at high speed until very smooth and light, about 3 minutes. As each batch is pureed, transfer it to a clean, large saucepan. Place the soup over medium-low heat, pour in the milk and stir well. Bring the soup to a simmer and add the reserved cooked florets and 1-1/2 cups of the cheese. Stir well until the cheese melts and the soup ois hot, about 5 minutes. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  5. Ladle the soup into warmed bowls. Sprinkle evenly with the remaining 1/2 cup cheese and the green onions. Serve immediately.
