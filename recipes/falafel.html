Falafel

Ingredients

  • 1 cup(s) chickpeas canned or rehydrated
  • 0.5 unit(s) onion(s) chopped roughly
  • 2 tablespoon(s) parsley finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoon(s) cilantro chopped
  • 1 teaspoon(s) salt
  • 1 teaspoon(s) chili pepper dried
  • 4 clove(s) garlic
  • 1 teaspoon(s) cumin seeds pulverized
  • 1 teaspoon(s) baking powder
  • 6 tablespoon(s) all-purpose flour

Directions

  1. Put the chickpeas in a large bowl and add enough cold water to cover them by at least 2 inches. Let soak overnight, then drain. Or use canned chickpeas, drained.
  2. Place the drained, uncooked chickpeas and the onions in the bowl of a food processor fitted with a steel blade. Add the parsley, cilantro, salt, hot pepper, garlic, and cumin.
  3. Process until blended but not pureed.
  4. Sprinkle in the baking powder and 4 tablespoons of the flour, and pulse. You want to add enough bulgur or flour so that the dough forms a small ball and no longer sticks to your hands.
  5. Turn into a bowl and refrigerate, covered, for several hours.
  6. Form the chickpea mixture into balls about the size of walnuts.
  7. Heat 3 inches of oil to 375F in a deep pot or wok and fry 1 ball to test. If it falls apart, add a little flour to the falafel mixture.
  8. Fry about 6 balls at once for a few minutes on each side, or until golden brown.
  9. Drain on paper towels.
  10. To serve: stuff half a pita with falafel balls, chopped tomatoes, onion, green pepper, and pickled turnips. Drizzle with tahini sauce.

Notes

Egyptians omit the cilantro and substitute fava beans for the chickpeas.
