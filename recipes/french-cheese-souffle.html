French Cheese Soufflé

Ingredients

  • 375 gram(s) heavy cream
  • 45 gram(s) butter
  • 40 gram(s) cornstarch or potato starch
  • 9 unit(s) egg(s) separated
  • 40 gram(s) parmeggiano grated
  • 185 gram(s) swiss cheese grated
  • 1 pinch nutmeg grated

Directions

    Notes

    In a pot over medium heat put the butter (cut up in small squares - it blends in faster), the cream and the starch - keep stirring until the mixture thickens, remove from flame. Quickly add the egg yolks and stir then add the cheeses and continue stirring. Add the nutmeg. If you used salted butter you do not need to add salt - otherwise, add some salt and then pepper. Now you have the basic mixture. In a Bol à Blanc (any large round bowl will do) start beating the egg whites with a large wisk (I prefer these to the blender which has a tendency to beat the whites too firm and grainy). Check once in a while - the whites should peak on the end of your whisk and stand up straight. Once the egg whites are beaten firm it is time to add the basic mixture which you have set aside. This is the delicate side of a successful soufflé. I pour the mixture directly into the whites and stir in a circular movement - using a flat spatula to do this. (Do not worry if the mixture has not blended completely.) [note - be somewhat gentle here and use the spatula to fold the mixture - as noted, the mix isn't homogenous]. Pour into a well-buttered soufflé bowl. Fill to the top and place in a pre-heated medium oven (160C/350F) for 40 minutes more or less. Check once in a while to see how the soufflé is doing. When ready, serve immediately remembering: On doit attendre un soufflé...mais, un soufflé ne doit jamais attendre! (One must wait for a soufflé but a soufflé MUST NEVER WAIT !!!)
