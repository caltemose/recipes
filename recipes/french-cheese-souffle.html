French Cheese Soufflé

Ingredients

  • 375 gram(s) heavy cream
  • 45 gram(s) butter
  • 40 gram(s) cornstarch or potato starch
  • 9 unit(s) egg(s) separated
  • 40 gram(s) parmeggiano grated
  • 185 gram(s) swiss cheese grated
  • 1 pinch nutmeg grated

Directions

  1. Preheat an oven to 160C/350F.
  2. Melt the butter in a pot over medium heat (cut up in small squares - it blends in faster).
  3. Add the cream and the starch. Keep stirring until the mixture thickens then remove from heat.
  4. Quickly add the egg yolks and stir then add the cheeses and continue stirring.
  5. Add the nutmeg. If you used salted butter you do not need to add salt - otherwise, add some salt and then pepper.
  6. In a Bol à Blanc (any large round bowl will do) start beating the egg whites with a large wisk.
  7. Check once in a while - the whites should peak on the end of your whisk and stand up straight.
  8. Add the basic mixture which you have set aside. This is the delicate side of a successful soufflé.
  9. Pour the mixture directly into the whites and stir in a circular movement - using a flat spatula to do this. Be gentle and do not worry if the mixture has not blended completely.
  10. Pour into a well-buttered soufflé bowl.
  11. Fill to the top and place in a pre-heated medium oven (160C/350F) for 40 minutes more or less.
  12. Check (through the oven window) once in a while to see how the soufflé is doing. Do not open the oven to do this.
  13. When ready, serve immediately remembering: "On doit attendre un soufflé...mais, un soufflé ne doit jamais attendre!" (One must wait for a soufflé but a soufflé must never wait!)
