Sauteed Chicken with Moroccan Hot and Sweet Tomato Sauce

Culinary Institute of America

Ingredients

  • 1 unit(s) onion(s) chopped
  • 2 clove(s) garlic finely minced
  • 2 butter
  • 0.75 teaspoon(s) ground cinnamon
  • 0.25 teaspoon(s) ground ginger
  • 0.25 teaspoon(s) cayenne pepper
  • 2 cup(s) tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoon(s) honey dark
  • 8 unit(s) chicken breasts boneless, skinless
  • 2 tablespoon(s) olive oil
  • 2 tablespoon(s) sesame seeds
  • 2 tablespoon(s) cilantro chopped

Directions

  1. In a food processor, process onion and garlic into a coarse paste. In a saucepan, heat the butter over medium heat. Add the onion puree and saute for about 10 minutes until tender.&nbsp; Stir in spices and saute for 2-3 minutes. Add tomato sauce and honey and simmer for 5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  2. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Blot chicken dry with paper towels and season with salt and pepper. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Add chicken pieces to pan and saute on one side for 2-3 minutes until browned. Turn chicken over and saute on second side for 3 minutes until browned. Reduce heat if necessary to avoid scorching chicken. Transfer chicken to baking dish.
  3. Spoon sauce over chicken and bake for 15 minutes or until chicken is cooked through (internal temp should be at least 180 degrees F).
  4. Divide chicken among serving plates and garnish with sesame seeds and cilantro.
