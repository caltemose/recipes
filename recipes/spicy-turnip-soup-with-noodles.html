Spicy Turnip Soup with Noodles

Soup cookbook

Ingredients

  • 2 unit(s) carrot(s) julienned
  • 1 unit(s) red bell pepper(s) seeded, julienned
  • 4 unit(s) scallions chopped
  • 2 unit(s) turnip(s) julienned
  • 0.5 teaspoon(s) red pepper flakes
  • 1 unit(s) jalapeño pepper(s) seeded and cut into thin rings (or try 1 dried chile, cut in half and seeded)
  • 2 unit(s) star anise
  • 2 teaspoon(s) tomato puree
  • 1.5 quart(s) vegetable stock hot
  • 1 packet(s) egg noodles
  • 1 teaspoon(s) soy sauce 1-2 teaspoons
  • 1 unit(s) cilantro small handful, chopped, for garnish
  • 1 unit(s) lime(s) cut into wedges

Directions

  1. Saute the carrots in olive oil for a few minutes in a large saucepan. Add scallions, turnips, bell pepper, pepper flakes, jalapeño, star anise, tomato puree and stock and bring to a boil.&nbsp; Lower the heat, partially cover with a lid and simmer gently for 15 or 20 minutes until turnips are tender. Discard the star anise (and dried chile if using).
  2. Meanwhile, put the noodles in a bowl, cover with boiling water, and let stand for 5 minutes, stirring to loosen, then drain. Stir noodles into the soup. Season to taste with soy sauce and pepper. Ladle into soup bowls, top with cilantro and a squeeze of lime juice.
