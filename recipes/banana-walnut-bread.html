Banana Walnut Bread

Ingredients

  • 1.25 cup(s) all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon(s) baking soda
  • 0.5 teaspoon(s) salt
  • 2 unit(s) egg(s) at room temperature
  • 0.5 teaspoon(s) vanilla extract
  • 0.5 cup(s) butter at room temperature
  • 1 cup(s) sugar
  • 3 unit(s) banana(s) very ripe, peeled, mashed
  • 0.5 cup(s) walnuts chopped into pieces

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350F.
  2. Sift the flour, baking soda, and salt into a medium bowl, set aside.
  3. Whisk the eggs and vanilla together in a liquid measuring cup with a spout, set aside.
  4. Lightly brush a 9 by 5 by 3-inch loaf pan with butter.
  5. In a standing mixer fitted with the paddle attachment or with an electric hand-held mixer, cream the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  6. Gradually pour the egg mixture into the butter while mixing until incorporated.
  7. Add the bananas (the mixture will appear to be curdled, so don't worry), and remove the bowl from the mixer.
  8. With a rubber spatula, mix in the flour mixture until just incorporated. Fold in the nuts and transfer the batter to the prepared pan.
  9. Bake for 55 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the bread comes out clean.
  10. Cool the bread in the pan on a wire rack for 5 minutes.
  11. Turn the bread out of the pan and let cool completely on the rack.

Notes

Sometimes I'll add a 4th banana (whole) to the top of the batter right (line it right down the middle) before baking the bread. Banana bread does not have a long shelf life so eat it within 3 days.
