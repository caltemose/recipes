Amaranth Wafers

Alton Brown

Ingredients

  • 100 gram(s) amaranth whole
  • 195 gram(s) brown sugar
  • 55 gram(s) butter room temp
  • 75 gram(s) amaranth flour
  • 3 gram(s) orange peel dried
  • 0.25 teaspoon(s) salt
  • 0.12 teaspoon(s) baking powder
  • 1 unit(s) egg(s)
  • 0.5 teaspoon(s) vanilla extract
  • 30 gram(s) orange peel, candied

Directions

  1. Heat an 8-inch nonstick skillet over medium-high heat for 2 minutes.&nbsp; Add 1 T of whole amaranth and shake into an even layer.&nbsp; Cover the pan and let the amaranth pop for 1 minute or until popping slows.&nbsp; Immediately remove to a bowl and continue popping the remaining amaranth 1 T at a time.&nbsp; When finished, allow to cool.
  2. Place brown sugar and butter in a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment and cream on medium speed for 2 minutes.
  3. Whisk the amaranth flour, dried orange peel, salt and baking powder together and set aside.
  4. Slow the mixer and beat in the egg and vanilla.&nbsp; Slowly add the flour mixture, followed by the popped amaranth and candied orange peel.&nbsp; Chill the batter for 30 minutes.
  5. Heat even to 325 degrees F.
  6. Drop the batter by the tablespoonful onto parchment paper-lined half sheet pans, 12 to a pan.&nbsp; Bake for 10-12 minutes.&nbsp; Cool for 1 minute on the pans before moving to racks to cool completely.&nbsp; Store in airtight container for up to 2 weeks.&nbsp; Can also freeze.
