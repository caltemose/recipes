Brined Chicken

Charcuterie

Ingredients

  • 4 liter(s) water
  • 1 cup(s) salt
  • 0.5 cup(s) sugar
  • 1 bunch tarragon
  • 1 bunch parsley
  • 2 unit(s) bay leaf
  • 1 head(s) garlic halved horizontally
  • 1 unit(s) onion(s) sliced
  • 3 tablespoon(s) peppercorn(s) lightly crushed
  • 2 unit(s) lemon(s) halved
  • 1 unit(s) chicken 3-5 pounds

Directions

  1. Combine all the brine ingredients in a pot large enough to hold the chicken. Give the lemons a good squeeze as you add them. Bring to a simmer and dissolve the salt and sugar.
  2. Remove from heat and let cool fully.
  3. Add the chicken to the brine and weigh it down so it remains completely submerged. Place in the refrigerator for 8-12 hours.
  4. Remove the chicken, rinse and pat dry and let rest uncovered in the refrigerator for 3 to 24 hours.
  5. Preheat the oven to 450F.
  6. Roast the chicken until it reaches an internal temperature of 160F.
  7. Remove from the oven and let it rest for at least 15 minutes before serving.

Notes

Do not shortcut the brining and resting times. I always omit the sugar and vary the ingredients here based on what's available. If you're going to get creative with the ingredients to flavor the chicken, be heavy-handed. I've substituted some of the water for beer and wine (not together obviously) and not seen a perceivable difference in the flavor of the chicken. In fact, the thing that seems to influence the flavor of the chicken the most is the quality of your chicken.
