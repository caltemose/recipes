Doro Wat (Ethiopian Chicken Stew)

Nom Nom Paleo

Ingredients

  • 3 pound(s) chicken drumsticks/thighs
  • 0.25 cup(s) ghee
  • 2 unit(s) red onion(s) sliced thinly
  • 1 unit(s) ginger fresh, about 1 inch grated finely
  • 1 tablespoon(s) berbere spices
  • 0.25 teaspoon(s) cardamom grounded
  • 0.25 teaspoon(s) black pepper grounded
  • 2 cup(s) chicken broth
  • 4 unit(s) egg(s) hard boiled

Directions

  1. Season chicken with salt and set aside.
  2. Melt the ghee in a large Dutch over medium heat.
  3. Add the sliced onions and cook undisturbed for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Season liberally with salt and gently turn over the pile of onions every 3 to 4 minutes to ensure even cooking.
  5. Once the onions have softened and reduced in volume, after about 15 minutes, turn the heat to medium low.
  6. Continue cooking for 30 to 40 more minutes, turning the onion pile every 10 minutes or so until they are nicely caramelized.
  7. Stir the ginger into the onions and cook for 30 seconds.
  8. Add the berbere, cardamom and black pepper then pour in the chicken broth and stir to mix well.
  9. Nestle the drumsticks into the liquid, and bring the contents of the pot to a low simmer.
  10. Cover and cook, turning the drumsticks occasionally, for 45 minutes or until the meat is tender and cooked through.
  11. In the meantime, make and peel the hard-boiled eggs.
  12. When the chicken is done, remove the lid and turn the heat to medium-high and cook for 5-10 minutes to reduce the liquid.
  13. Add optional juice of a lime.
  14. Transfer the chicken to a serving dish, garnish with egg wedges and ladle sauce on top to serve.

Notes

The original recipe adds 3 minced garlic cloves with the ginger.
