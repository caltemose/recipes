Chicken Broth

Ingredients

  • 1 unit(s) chicken cleaned
  • 2 stalk(s) celery broken into 3 pieces
  • 2 unit(s) carrot(s) cut in half
  • 1 unit(s) onion(s) cut in quarters
  • 1 pinch peppercorn(s) to taste
  • 4 quart(s) water cold

Directions

  1. Put the chicken in a stock pot. Add the celery, carrots, and onion.
  2. Cook on medium heat for a little while to lightly brown the ingredients.
  3. Add water and whatever seasonings you like - at a minimum I'll add peppercorns and bay leaves.
  4. Cover the pot and simmer for at least an hour.
  5. When done, cool and store in the fridge or freeze it.

Notes

Mason jars are ideal for storage when not freezing the broth. When freezing use ziplocks. / The yield on this recipe varies based on how long you reduce your broth. / If you want to remove the fat, let the stock cool then refrigerate for a few hours to solidify the fat. It can then be removed easily. / If you buy chicken specifically for broth, make sure the parts vary. If you try to make broth solely out of the cheaper unpopular bits (necks, backs, etc) then you're likely to end up with a very fatty broth that may not have as much flavor as you want (if you were to use those parts, you're best off roasting them for a while before making the stock).
