Chicken Wild Rice Soup

family recipe

Ingredients

  • 0.5 cup(s) butter
  • 1 unit(s) onion(s) finely chopped
  • 0.5 cup(s) celery chopped
  • 0.5 cup(s) carrot(s) sliced
  • 0.5 pound(s) mushrooms sliced
  • 0.75 cup(s) all-purpose flour
  • 2 cup(s) wild rice cooked
  • 6 cup(s) chicken broth
  • 1 pound(s) chicken cooked
  • 0.5 teaspoon(s) salt
  • 0.5 teaspoon(s) curry powder
  • 0.5 teaspoon(s) mustard powder
  • 0.5 teaspoon(s) parsley dried
  • 0.5 teaspoon(s) black pepper
  • 1 cup(s) almonds slivered
  • 3 tablespoon(s) sherry dry
  • 2 cup(s) half and half

Directions

  1. Melt butter in a large saucepan over medium heat. Stir in onion, celery and carrots and saute for 5 minutes. Add mushrooms and saute for 2 more minutes. Then add flour and stir well. Gradually pour in chicken broth, stirring constantly, until all has been added. Bring just to a boil, reduce heat to low and simmer.
  2. Next, add rice, chicken, salt, curry powder, mustard powder, parsley, black pepper, almonds and sherry. Allow to heat through, then pour in half-and-half. Let simmer for 1-2 hours. (Note: do not boil or your roux will break.)
