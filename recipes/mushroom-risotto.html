Mushroom Risotto

Ingredients

  • 1 pound(s) mushrooms
  • 5.5 cup(s) Chicken Broth or veg, or mushroom liquor
  • 6 tablespoon(s) butter
  • 1 unit(s) onion(s) chopped
  • 1 unit(s) garlic finely chopped
  • 2 cup(s) arborio rice
  • 0.5 cup(s) wine red or dry white
  • 2 tablespoon(s) parsley
  • 0.5 cup(s) parmeggiano

Directions

    Notes

    Heat the broth. Remove any dirt from the mushrooms and trim the tough bottoms of the stems. If the mushrooms are large, halve or quarter them lengthwise. Melt 4 tablespoons of butter in a skillet over medium heat. Add the mushrooms and sauté until tender, about 10 minutes. The cooking time will depend on the variety of mushrooms. Remove from heat and set aside. In a large skillet over medium heat, melt the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until softened but not colored, about 5 minutes. Add the rice and sauté over medium-hight heat for 1 to 2 minutes, stirring to coat the grains well. Stir in the wine, reduce the heat to medium, and allow it to evaporate and become absorbed, about 3 minutes. Now add a ladleful of the hot broth, stirring well. When it is nearly all absorbed into the rice, add another ladle of the hot broth, continuing to stir. Continue in this fashion until the broth is absorbed by and the rice is half-cooked (about 15 minutes), stirring continuously. Stir in half the mushrooms. Continue cooking the rice as described, adding a ladleful of hot broth at a time, stirring all the while and waiting until it is absorbed before adding the next ladleful. When the rice is tender but still slightly chewy - it should be creamy but not mushy - remove from the heat (you may not need to use all the broth). The total cooking time of the rice is from 20 to 25 minutes. Stir in the remaining mushrooms, the parsley and the 1/2 cup cheese. Add salt and pepper. Serve hot.
